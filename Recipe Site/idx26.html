Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/4 cup milk (as needed for dough)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods
  • Oil or ghee (for frying)
  • Chopped pistachios/almonds (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  2. Add ghee and mix well until crumbly. Gradually add milk to make a soft dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small smooth balls without cracks.
  4. Heat oil/ghee on medium-low flame and fry the balls until golden brown.
  5. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, and cardamom until slightly sticky. Soak fried balls in warm syrup for at least 1-2 hours.

Kadhai Paneer

Ingredients

  • 250 g paneer (cubed)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 large capsicum (sliced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds (crushed)
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Recipe

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a kadhai (wok) and add cumin seeds.
  2. Add crushed coriander seeds, then sauté onions until golden.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cook until raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  5. Add capsicum slices and sauté for 2–3 minutes to keep them slightly crunchy.
  6. Mix in paneer cubes, garam masala, and kasuri methi. Stir gently to coat paneer with masala.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
